# NoblePort Content Strategy

This is a strong framework. For NoblePort specifically, three changes increase the odds of building a real audience instead of an AI-content factory.

## What Fits NoblePort Best

### Channel 1 — Construction Reality

- Jobsite transformations
- Structural repairs
- Basement rebuilds
- Roofing failures
- Permit challenges
- Before/after walkthroughs
- Cost breakdowns

Examples:

- "Why This Basement Was About to Collapse"
- "The $100,000 Fix Nobody Wanted to Hear"
- "What We Found Behind This Wall Changed Everything"

### Channel 2 — New England Real Estate

- Historic homes
- Coastal properties
- Development opportunities
- ADU projects
- Zoning and permitting

Examples:

- "Why This 1850 Newburyport Home Is Still Standing"
- "The Hidden Cost of Buying Coastal Property"
- "Can You Build an ADU on This Lot?"

### Channel 3 — NoblePort Systems

- Construction AI
- PermitStream
- PMagent
- GCagent
- Real business operations

Examples:

- "Can AI Run a Construction Company?"
- "How We Track Every Permit in Essex County"
- "The Construction Command Center We Built"

### Script Layer

Use:

- Value-add commentary
- CTA

### Voice Layer

Best options:

- ElevenLabs
- Krisp
- Your own voice

For monetization safety, your own voice remains the strongest option.

### Visual Layer

- Higgsfield
- Canva
- Drone footage
- Jobsite footage
- Time-lapses

The highest-performing NoblePort content will likely be actual jobsite footage mixed with AI visuals, not fully AI-generated visuals.

### Editing Layer

- CapCut
- Adobe Premiere Pro

Target:

- Visual change every 3–5 seconds
- Pattern interrupts every 20–30 seconds
- New hook every minute
